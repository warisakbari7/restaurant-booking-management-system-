# 3. Business Problem Statement

## 3.1 Introduction

Bella Vista Restaurant currently relies on manual processes to manage table reservations and customer bookings. Reservations are primarily recorded using handwritten notes and telephone conversations. While this approach has been adequate for a small number of daily reservations, it no longer meets the operational needs of a growing restaurant.

The increasing number of customers, reservations, and daily operational activities has made manual reservation management inefficient, error-prone, and difficult to scale.

---

## 3.2 Current Problems

The restaurant faces several business challenges that directly affect operational efficiency and customer satisfaction.

### Reservation Management

* Reservations are recorded manually.
* Staff must search reservation records manually.
* Reservation updates require manual corrections.
* There is no centralized reservation database.

### Table Management

* Staff cannot view real-time table availability.
* Table assignments depend on staff experience.
* Tables may remain unused despite customer demand.
* Double-booking can occur during busy periods.

### Customer Management

* Customer information is not stored centrally.
* Reservation history cannot be reviewed efficiently.
* Returning customers cannot be identified easily.
* Customer preferences are not recorded.

### Operational Management

* Managers have limited visibility into daily operations.
* Reservation statistics are unavailable.
* Peak-hour planning is difficult.
* Performance reporting requires manual effort.

---

## 3.3 Business Impact

These problems negatively affect multiple aspects of restaurant operations.

### Customer Impact

* Longer booking times.
* Increased risk of reservation errors.
* Reduced customer confidence.
* Poor overall booking experience.

### Staff Impact

* Increased administrative workload.
* More time spent answering phone calls.
* Difficulty managing reservations during busy hours.
* Greater likelihood of human error.

### Business Impact

* Inefficient use of restaurant capacity.
* Missed revenue opportunities.
* Reduced operational efficiency.
* Limited ability to analyze business performance.

---

## 3.4 Root Cause Analysis

The primary causes of these issues include:

* Lack of a centralized reservation management system.
* Heavy reliance on manual processes.
* No real-time table availability tracking.
* Limited access to operational data.
* Absence of standardized reservation workflows.

---

## 3.5 Proposed Business Solution

To address these challenges, Bella Vista Restaurant requires a centralized web-based Restaurant Booking Management System (RBMS).

The proposed system will:

* Digitize the reservation process.
* Maintain a centralized reservation database.
* Display real-time table availability.
* Reduce booking conflicts.
* Improve customer communication.
* Provide operational dashboards for administrators.
* Support business reporting and future system expansion.

---

## 3.6 Expected Outcomes

Following implementation, the restaurant expects to achieve:

* Faster reservation processing.
* Fewer booking errors.
* Improved table utilization.
* Better customer satisfaction.
* Reduced administrative workload.
* Improved visibility into restaurant operations.
* A scalable platform capable of supporting future enhancements.
