# 4. Business Objectives

## 4.1 Introduction

The Restaurant Booking Management System (RBMS) is designed to modernize the reservation process and improve the daily operations of Bella Vista Restaurant. The business objectives defined in this chapter establish the expected outcomes of the project and provide measurable goals that will guide the design, development, and evaluation of the system.

---

## 4.2 Primary Business Objective

The primary objective of the Restaurant Booking Management System is to replace the restaurant's manual reservation process with a centralized, secure, and user-friendly digital platform that improves operational efficiency and enhances the customer booking experience.

---

## 4.3 Business Goals

The project aims to achieve the following business goals:

### BO-01 – Digitalize Reservation Management

Replace handwritten reservation records and phone-based booking processes with an online reservation system that stores reservation data securely in a centralized database.

**Expected Benefit**

* Faster reservation processing
* Reduced paperwork
* Improved data accuracy

---

### BO-02 – Improve Table Utilization

Enable restaurant staff to manage table availability more effectively by providing real-time visibility into table occupancy and reservation schedules.

**Expected Benefit**

* Better use of available tables
* Reduced idle capacity
* Increased seating efficiency

---

### BO-03 – Enhance Customer Experience

Provide customers with a simple and convenient method to search for available reservation times, create bookings, modify reservations, and cancel bookings when necessary.

**Expected Benefit**

* Faster booking experience
* Improved customer satisfaction
* Increased customer confidence

---

### BO-04 – Reduce Operational Errors

Minimize reservation conflicts and human errors by validating bookings automatically and preventing double-booking of tables.

**Expected Benefit**

* Fewer reservation mistakes
* Greater booking accuracy
* More reliable reservation management

---

### BO-05 – Improve Administrative Efficiency

Provide restaurant administrators with centralized tools to manage reservations, tables, customers, restaurant settings, and operational information.

**Expected Benefit**

* Reduced administrative workload
* Faster daily operations
* Improved management efficiency

---

### BO-06 – Support Business Reporting

Collect operational data that can be used to generate reports and support business decision-making.

Examples include:

* Daily reservations
* Reservation cancellations
* Table occupancy
* Customer activity

**Expected Benefit**

* Better operational planning
* Improved business insights
* Data-driven decision-making

---

### BO-07 – Provide a Scalable Foundation

Develop a system architecture that supports future enhancements such as online payments, loyalty programs, multiple restaurant branches, QR code ordering, and mobile applications.

**Expected Benefit**

* Easier future expansion
* Reduced redevelopment costs
* Long-term maintainability

---

## 4.4 Success Indicators

The project will be considered successful if it achieves the following outcomes:

* Reservations can be completed online without staff assistance.
* Reservation conflicts are significantly reduced.
* Restaurant staff can view real-time table availability.
* Customers can manage their reservations through the system.
* Administrators can manage restaurant operations using a centralized dashboard.
* Reservation data is stored securely and can be retrieved efficiently.
* The system provides accurate operational reports.

---

## 4.5 Development Impact

The objectives defined in this chapter provide direction for the remainder of the project.

They will directly influence:

* Functional Requirements (SRS)
* User Stories
* Database Design
* API Design
* User Interface Design
* Test Cases
* Acceptance Criteria

Every major feature implemented in the system should support one or more business objectives defined in this document.
